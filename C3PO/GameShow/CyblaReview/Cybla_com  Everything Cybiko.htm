   Gameshow Beta-Review

02/01/02-

Author: Greg Smith, DevCybiko

Introduction:

I think this review would start out nicely with a quote from Greg himself:

"A gameshow app was the first thing that came to mind when I first learned of the Cybiko. Now, I'm finally getting around to it."

Gameshow is yet another great innovation by Greg of DevCybiko. The name says it all. Gameshow is divided into two parts: the Master of Ceremonies (MC) and the Players. The MC is like the junction of A*Chat, and the Players are the clients. This application requires 3 Cybiko/CybikoXtreme's and B2C type b passwords for all.

The Review:

The MC:

The first screen you see after executing the MC is the number of minutes you want the gameshow to last. In this way you can specify an overall time limit for your players.

 


This is where you can input the number of seconds the player's have to answer the question you present.

 

 


After the two settings are submitted, a screen stating "Joining..." is shown. This is where the MC waits until at least two players have connected to the MC and have been allowed to play.

 


You can actually authorize if the player who tried to connect can play or not!

 

 


After the players have connected and authorized, the MC displays the list of players and you can now proceed to the main of the MC by pressing enter.

 

 


Someone rings in and answers the question you have asked. You simply select ok if they are right and cancel if they are wrong. It will then tell the player if he is right or wrong.

 


As you can see, the score is displayed beside the player's name.

 

 

 


After a question is finished, it asks you to input the number of points the next question is worth. The default is 100.

 

 


Talk about domination =)

 

 


The Client:

The first you get after executing the client is the "Select your MC" screen. Select the Cybiko/CybikoXtreme running the MC application here.

 

 

Enter your name.

 

 

 


Once your name is in place, you are now ready to answer questions asked my the user of the MC.

 

 


Know the answer? Press enter to buzz in quick!

 

 

 

Say the answer aloud to the user of the MC. If you're right, the user will press OK in the MC.

 

 


And credited with the points!

 

 

 


But there will be days when your answer is wrong. =)

 

 

Conclusion:

No more resorting to bulky light/buzzer combos and flashcards, Gameshow is the app that will be a big hit with your friends and in family get-togethers. Now go tell Greg what an awesome application this is so he will release it when it is completed!

-Prestone
